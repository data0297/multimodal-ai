# Guest speakers

## Application of Machine Learning on Audio and More
>Speaker: Sile Yin, Senior ML engineer at Bose Research  
Time: April 13, 2026, 6-7pm   
Location: Anderson Wing TTC, Room 208, Medford campus, Tufts University   

In this talk, we are going to cover some of the latest works of the application of machine learning on audio. We will focus on speech enhancement as an example. We start by revisiting basics of digital signal processing that are still relevant in the age of deep neural networks. Then we will walk through the history in the development of speech enhancement, quickly walk through challenges in those traditional methods and how later methods have solved those, and how machine learning and deep neural networks revolutionize the field. Moreover, we are going to look at methods that incorporate other modalities to improve or solve problems that are difficult with audio only approaches. Specifically, we will introduce the recent work Bose Research conducted in audio-visual enhancement. Other than speech enhancement, we will also take a brief look at other applications of machine learning on audio that Bose Research has worked on.

Bio: Sile Yin is a Senior Machine Learning Research Engineer in Bose Research, focusing on deep learning and DSP for real-time audio applications. Sile holds a M.S in Music Technology from Georgia Institute of Technology and a B.S. in Physics/Acoustics from Nanjing University. Sile has published and presented her research at venues including Interspeech, WASPAA, Acoustic Society of America, and Harvard University. She also holds multiple patents in the area of deep learning based dialog enhancement, audio-visual speech enhancement, and IMU based VAD.
